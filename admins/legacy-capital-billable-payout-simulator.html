How the deduction & payout model works

Payments 1 & 2 — draft fee deducted only
For the first two payments, only the $4 draft/processing fee is backed out before calculating affiliate revenue. Monthly schedule: deduct $4. Split schedule: deduct $4 × 2 = $8 (one per installment). The remaining net amount is multiplied by the pay rate tier to arrive at the affiliate payout.
Payments 3+ — maintenance backout + draft fee
From payment 3 onward, both the maintenance fee and the draft fee are backed out before the pay rate is applied. Monthly: deduct $80 (or $120) + $4 = $84 (or $124). Split: deduct ($40/$60 + $4) × 2 = $88 (or $128) per month — each of the two installments backs out half the maintenance fee plus the $4 draft fee. This applies from payment 3 through payment 48, and continues the same rate beyond payment 48 if the term extends.
Pay rate tiers — applied to net eligible amount every month
Tier 1 — 1 to 99 enrollments/month: 60% of net eligible amount.
Tier 2 — 100+ enrollments/month: 65% of net eligible amount.
The pay rate applies to every payment, including payments 1 and 2. Only the deduction differs between phases.
Why billable beats upfront — the affiliate compounding advantage
An upfront model earns 8% of enrolled debt once — then stops. The billable model generates monthly revenue for the full program term on every active client. Consistent monthly enrollment creates a compounding stack of revenue streams that grows every month until steady state, where each month's inflow equals the total per-client payout multiplied by your enrollment volume.
