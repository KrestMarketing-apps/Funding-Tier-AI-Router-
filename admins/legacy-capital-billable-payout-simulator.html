How the billable payout model works for affiliates

Months 1 & 2 — 100% pass-through + maintenance fee
The affiliate receives 100% of the service fee component plus the maintenance fee ($80 or $120) for the first two months. This front-loads meaningful cash while the program is newest and client retention is highest.
Month 3 onwards — pay rate share of service fee
From month 3, the affiliate earns their pay rate (60% for under 100 enrollments/month, 65% for 100+) applied to the monthly service fee installment. Maintenance fees from month 3 onward are allocated to the ELP. Processing fees ($4/month) do not flow to the affiliate.
Pay rate tiers
Tier 1 — 1 to 99 enrollments/month: 60% of the monthly service fee installment.
Tier 2 — 100+ enrollments/month: 65% of the monthly service fee installment.
Why billable beats upfront — the affiliate compounding advantage
With an upfront model, your affiliate company earns 8% of enrolled debt once — then the revenue stops. With the billable model, every active client continues generating monthly revenue through their entire program term. Consistent monthly enrollment creates a compounding stack of revenue streams that grows every month until it reaches steady state — where each month's inflow equals the total per-client payout multiplied by your monthly enrollment volume. This is what makes Legacy Capital's model uniquely valuable for affiliate partners building long-term recurring revenue.
